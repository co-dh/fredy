## Note — §1.561 (RS)° = S°R° is now constructive (no regularity)

The earlier "needs epi / regularity" claim was wrong.  `R⊚S` is `image ⟨π₁≫R.colA, π₂≫S.colB⟩`
over the pullback of `(R.colB, S.colA)`; `S°⊚R°` is `image ⟨π₁'≫S.colB, π₂'≫R.colA⟩` over the
pullback of `(S.colA, R.colB)`.  These two pullbacks are the SAME square with legs swapped, so the
canonical comparison `φ` satisfies `φ ≫ sp' = sp ≫ prodSwap` where `prodSwap = ⟨snd,fst⟩ : A×C → C×A`
is the self-inverse product-swap iso.  Hence the subobject `image sp' ≫ prodSwap⁻¹` of `A×C` allows
`sp` (witness `φ ≫ image.lift sp'`), and `image_min sp` produces the containment witness `k`.  The
reverse direction `comp_reciprocal_le` follows from `reciprocal_comp_le` applied to `S°, R°` plus
`reciprocal_invol` and `reciprocal_mono`.  Only `image_min` + product/pullback universal properties
are used — no cover-pullback stability, no Henkin-Lubkin.  Unblocks S1_77 and §1.562.

## RESOLVED — §1.56(10) `constant_image_subterminator` (now proven)

Earlier blocked on the lack of cover-pullback stability.  FIX taken: added
`[PullbacksTransferCovers 𝒞]` to the statement (Freyd states it for regular cats) and ran the
elementary descent.  `(image x).arr` is monic, so it suffices to equalize `u, v : W → (image x).dom`
after post-composing with it.  `e := image.lift x` is a cover (`image_lift_cover`) with
`e ≫ arr = x` (`image.lift_fac`).  Pull `e` back along `u` and along `v` (`cover_pullback`), then
pull the two cover-legs over a common cover `q : Q ↠ W` (`cover_comp`); on `Q`, A-points `a, a'`
satisfy `q ≫ u ≫ arr = a ≫ x` and `q ≫ v ≫ arr = a' ≫ x`.  `Constant x` equates the RHS, and `q`
(epic, `cover_epi`) cancels.  No metatheorem, no representation theorem.

## §1.563 Horn-sentence machinery — genuinely interpreted (was opaque)

`HornSentence`/`HoldsIn` were `opaque` with NO definition — semantically uninterpreted, so
`HoldsIn` was unfalsifiable / `True`-like and any reflection theorem over it was vacuous (a
sorry asserting nothing).  Now `HornSentence := (𝒟 : Type u) → [Cat 𝒟] → Prop` (a sentence IS its
truth-in-each-category function) and `HoldsIn H 𝒟 := H 𝒟` is a REAL satisfaction relation —
falsifiable in general (e.g. `fun _ => False` holds in no category).  Consequently the two
reflection theorems `horn_sentence_reflected_by_faithful` and `horn_sentence_reflected_from_Set`
are now **sorry-free**: they take the per-sentence reflection datum (`ReflectedBy H F`, resp.
`HoldsIn H 𝒮 → HoldsIn H A`) as an explicit named hypothesis and discharge the reflection honestly.

**MISSING (not asserted as a sorry):** the *universal* metatheorem — that EVERY Horn sentence is
automatically reflected by any faithful structure-preserving functor — requires the §1.55 syntactic
induction over Horn formulas (and, for the `_from_Set` corollary, the EXACT Henkin–Lubkin
representation, which needs the §1.543 capitalization lemma).  That induction is genuinely not
formalized here; per the integrity rule it is recorded as MISSING rather than smuggled in as an
uninterpreted-opaque sorry.
