# Pre-logos in Chapter 2 (Allegories)

A **pre-logos** is a regular category in which each lattice of subobjects `𝓢𝓾𝓫(A)` is a lattice (§1.6).
Equivalently it is one **rung** on the ladder of category types Freyd builds up — a step above regular
categories, below logos:

```
cartesian → regular → pre-logos → logos → topos
```

Chapter 2 ("Allegories") mirrors this ladder against a parallel ladder of allegories, and pre-logos is the
rung that lines up with **distributive allegories**:

| category side        | allegory side                  |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| regular category     | unitary tabular allegory       |
| **pre-logos**        | **distributive allegory**      |
| logos                | division allegory              |
| topos                | power allegory                 |

The whole game of Chapter 2 is the correspondence

> category C  ⟷  its allegory of relations 𝓡𝓮𝓵(C),  and back via the maps functor 𝓜𝓪𝓹(A)

## How pre-logos is used

**1. The defining correspondence (§2.212–2.213).**
If C is a pre-logos then 𝓡𝓮𝓵(C) is a *distributive* allegory (its lattices of subobjects give the
union/∅ structure, §1.616). Conversely, if A is a tabular unitary distributive allegory, then 𝓜𝓪𝓹(A) is a
pre-logos. So "pre-logos" = "maps of a distributive allegory." This also lets him build the **effective
reflection** of a pre-logos as the maps of the effective reflection of its allegory.

**2. Positivity ⟷ coproducts (§2.214–2.216).**
A pre-logos C is *positive* iff 𝓡𝓮𝓵(C) has finite coproducts (equivalently finite products, by
reciprocation). This is the allegory-theoretic characterization of positive pre-logos, and defines
"positive allegory."

**3. The promised representation theorems (§2.217), "as repeatedly promised in chapter 1":**
- *A pre-logos may be faithfully represented in a positive pre-logos* — built by taking the positive
  reflection A⁺ of its allegory of relations and forming the maps. (Discharges the forward references at
  §1.624, §1.628, §1.632, etc.)
- *A pre-logos may be faithfully represented in a pre-topos* (its reflection into pre-topoi), since a
  pre-topos is an effective positive pre-logos.

**4. The hierarchy ladder (§2.3).**
Pre-logos sits as one step in the chain above; §2.3 shows 𝓜𝓪𝓹 of the right kind of allegory is a logos
(a pre-logos satisfying the extra double-sharp axiom).

**5. Free objects / logic (Appendix B, §B.314–B.315).**
Applied to theories: a *coherent* theory yields a distributive allegory whose maps-category is a pre-logos;
the empty coherent theory gives the **free pre-logos** generated by the predicate symbols.

## Short answer

In Chapter 2 "pre-logos" is used as the precise categorical image of a tabular distributive allegory, and
it is the setting in which Freyd finally proves the Chapter-1 promises — faithful representation into a
positive pre-logos and into a pre-topos.
