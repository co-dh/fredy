- **`preTopos_functor_preserves_equalizers` (step ii)** — CLOSED, sorry-free in its own
  proof term.  Genuine reduction to step (i):
  1. The chosen equalizer `(E, m)` of `f, g` is, by `isEqualizer_iff_isPullback` (§1.434,
     S1_43), the pullback in 𝒜 of the graph maps `u = ⟨1,f⟩`, `v = ⟨1,g⟩ : A → A×B`.
  2. `u, v` are split monic (retraction `fst`, via `mono_of_retraction`), so step (i)
     (`preTopos_functor_preserves_monic_pullbacks`) carries that pullback to a pullback of
     `(F u, F v)` in ℬ — packaged by building a `HasPullback u v` whose distinguished cone
     IS the equalizer-as-pullback cone, so step (i)'s output lands on the right object.
  3. The product-comparison iso `φ = ⟨F fst, F snd⟩` (iso by `pres_products`) satisfies
     `F u ≫ φ = ⟨1,Ff⟩`, `F v ≫ φ = ⟨1,Fg⟩`; `isPullback_of_iso_cospan` rewrites the
     cospan to the canonical graph maps in ℬ.
  4. `isEqualizer_iff_isPullback` run backwards in ℬ gives `(F E, F m)` = equalizer of
     `(F f, F g)` — exactly the goal.
  The declaration carries no `sorry` (verified: build flags only lines 131/250, steps i/iii).

## Discharged previously

- **`coprod_is_pushout_of_init`** (private): A + B is the pushout of (0→A, 0→B). Proof
  directly from `HasBinaryCoproducts` + `HasCoterminator` axioms: any pushout cocone
  over the two initial maps automatically commutes by `init_uniq`, and `case` gives the
  mediating map.
- **Part (c) of `preTopos_functor_is_bicartesian_repr`**: coproduct preservation.
  Proof sketch: (1) use `coprod_is_pushout_of_init` to cast `coprod A B` as a pushout;
  (2) `PreservesInitial` makes any cocone over `(F∘init A, F∘init B)` automatically
  commute; (3) `PreservesPushouts` applied to that cocone gives the inverse map
  `inv : F(coprod A B) → coprod(FA,FB)`; (4) `fwd ≫ inv = id` by `case_uniq` on
  the inclusions; (5) `inv ≫ fwd = id` by pushout uniqueness applied to the self-cocone.
