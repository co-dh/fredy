## What §1.76 says

Given a point `x ∈ X` in a topological space, let `ℱ` be the filter of open sets containing `x`.
The category of **micro-sheaves over `x`** is the quotient logos `𝓜(X)/ℱ` (the `A/ℱ` construction of
§1.731). The section is a worked **counterexample**:

- Take `X = [0,1]` the unit interval, `x` its midpoint, `N` the discrete space of naturals.
- `N × X`, as an object of `𝓜(X)`, is a countable copower of the terminator and is the union of its
  points.
- In the micro-sheaf category `𝓜(X)/ℱ` it is *no longer* a copower nor the union of its points: by
  shrinking each open interval `Uₙ` around `x` by a factor `n` one builds a subobject `B'` that still
  contains all the points but for which **no** `V ∈ ℱ` makes `V × B' → V × B` an isomorphism. Hence
  there is no smallest subobject containing all points of `N × X`.
- Conclusion: this category of micro-sheaves has **no countable copowers** (else the union of points
  of `N × X` would be the image of a map `Σ 1 → N × X`).

§1.761 adds: if `ℱ` is closed under countable intersection then `A → A/ℱ` preserves countable unions
and coproducts; micro-sheaves over `x` are locally complete only when `x` has a minimal open
neighbourhood `U`, in which case `𝓜(X)/ℱ ≃ 𝓜(U)`; for a `T₁` space, `x` must then be isolated and
`𝓜(X)/ℱ ≃ Set`.

The only abstract, non-topological ingredient — the quotient logos `A/ℱ` of §1.731 — is **already**
present in `Fredy/S1_72.lean` (the §1.73 filter/quotient development). §1.76 contributes no new
faithfully-stateable definition or elementary lemma beyond it: every theorem here is about a
*specific topological* category `𝓜([0,1])` and its sheaf-theoretic structure. Stating any of it would
require a sheaves-on-a-space logos, which is outside the current `Cat`-based infrastructure (and
mathlib's topology/`CategoryTheory` is banned for this repo). Hence: documented MISSING, no file.
