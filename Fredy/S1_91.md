## Sorry explanations

**`relPullback_comp`** (§1.911): DISCHARGED. The equation g*(f*(R)) = (f≫g)*(R) is now proven
by hand from the pullback universal properties. Let `Pg = pullback(g, R.colA)`,
`Pf = pullback(f, Pg.π₁)` (= source of `relPullback f (relPullback g R)`), and
`Q = pullback(f≫g, R.colA)` (= source of `relPullback (f≫g) R`). Forward `RelHom` is
`Q.lift` of the cone `(Pf.π₁, Pf.π₂ ≫ Pg.π₂)`; backward is `Pf.lift` of the cone
`(Q.π₁, m)` where `m = Pg.lift (Q.π₁ ≫ f, Q.π₂)`. colB legs match by associativity. (Note:
S1_45's `invImg_comp` is the analogous *subobject*-level pasting and is still a sorry there,
but is out of scope for this file.)

**`omega_monic_endo_is_involution`** (§1.919): REMAINING faithful sorry — genuine infrastructure
blocker (confirmed by a deep proof-search attempt). To finish via the only available API
(`classify_unique`, S1_9) one must show `t : 1 → Ω` is the pullback of `t` along `g≫g`, i.e.
`g≫g` classifies the maximal subobject of Ω ("A is g²-large in itself", Freyd p.162). That
requires three pieces, none formalized here:
  1. `classify`-iso-invariance (classifying map depends only on the subobject up to iso) —
     easy from `classify_unique` (~30 lines).
  2. The **characterizing lemmas** for `omegaMeet`/`heytingDoubleArrow` (both *defined* in this
     file but with NO universal property): `omegaMeet_spec` ("A' ⊆ meet(A₁,A₂) ↔ A'⊆A₁ ∧ A'⊆A₂",
     i.e. the pullback of `t` along `⟨χ₁,χ₂⟩≫omegaMeet` ≅ `Sub.inter A₁ A₂`) and the analogous
     Heyting-double-arrow spec. This is the substantive missing construction — the bridge
     "internal-meet-on-Ω induces `Sub.inter` (S1_45)".
  3. **Operation-extensionality** ("if g,g' yield the same large subobjects they are equal"),
     STRICTLY STRONGER than `classify_unique`: the latter says one *fixed* monic has a unique
     classifier; it does NOT say two maps Ω→Ω agreeing on every A's large-subobject predicate
     coincide. Deriving it needs the full `Sub(−) ≅ Hom(−,Ω)` bijection (the `mem`/`powerClassify`/
     `IsUniversalRel` machinery for `[1]=Ω`) connected to `HasSubobjectClassifier.classify`.
  The statement is faithful to §1.919 and stays as-is.

**`minimal_topos_has_terminator`** (§1.91(10)): REMAINING faithful sorry — structural blocker.
The stated hypothesis is `[HasSubobjectClassifier 𝒞]`, which only provides a power-object for the
terminator (Ω = [1]). Freyd's construction explicitly uses, for every pair A,B, the relation
`M_{A,B}` tabulated by the product projection `A×B → A`, and the constant idempotent
`Λ(M_{B,B}) : [B] → [B]`, whose equalizer with `id_{[B]}` is the terminator. This needs
`HasPowerObject B` for ALL B, plus the `Λ/ε` classify-bijection at each `[B]` — neither present
(only `HasPowerObject` *structure* exists in S1_9, with no instances and not wired to the
classifier). The hypothesis is too weak to even express the construction; a faithful proof
requires strengthening to `[∀ B, HasPowerObject B]` and formalizing tabulation of `M_{A,B}`.
