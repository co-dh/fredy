## Sorry explanations

**`relPullback_comp`** (§1.911): DISCHARGED. The equation g*(f*(R)) = (f≫g)*(R) is now proven
by hand from the pullback universal properties. Let `Pg = pullback(g, R.colA)`,
`Pf = pullback(f, Pg.π₁)` (= source of `relPullback f (relPullback g R)`), and
`Q = pullback(f≫g, R.colA)` (= source of `relPullback (f≫g) R`). Forward `RelHom` is
`Q.lift` of the cone `(Pf.π₁, Pf.π₂ ≫ Pg.π₂)`; backward is `Pf.lift` of the cone
`(Q.π₁, m)` where `m = Pg.lift (Q.π₁ ≫ f, Q.π₂)`. colB legs match by associativity.

**`omega_monic_endo_is_involution`** (§1.919): REMAINING faithful sorry — genuine infrastructure
blocker. To finish via `classify_unique` (the only available extensionality tool from S1_9) one
must show `t : 1 → Ω` is the pullback of `t` along `g≫g`, i.e. the square
`t ≫ (g≫g) = term 1 ≫ t` commutes and is a pullback. Two concrete subgoals after
`rw [← classify_true_eq_id]; apply HasSubobjectClassifier.classify_unique`:

  1. **Square** (`hsq`): `true ≫ g ≫ g = term 1 ≫ true` — equivalent to `true ≫ g ≫ g = true`,
     i.e., `g` fixes `true : 1 → Ω`. Maps from the terminal `1` to Ω are not forced to be equal
     (the terminal UP only applies to maps TO `1`), so this needs the book's U/V argument.
  2. **IsPullback**: the cone `(1, true, term 1)` is a pullback of `(g≫g, true)`. Even if (1)
     holds, this needs: for any `(E, p, q)` with `p ≫ (g≫g) = q ≫ true`, there is unique
     `u : E → 1` with `u ≫ true = p`. This requires g² acting on `Sub(E)` to send every
     subobject to the identity — the full "large-subobject" machinery.

  Three root pieces, none formalized:
  - `classify`-iso-invariance: two classifiers of isomorphic monics coincide (~30 lines from
    `classify_unique`). Individually provable.
  - **characterizing lemmas** for `omegaMeet` / `heytingDoubleArrow`: the universal-property
    specs ("A' ⊆ meet(A₁,A₂) ↔ A'⊆A₁ ∧ A'⊆A₂") are not proven — the definitions exist but
    have no stated universal property. This is the main missing construction.
  - **operation-extensionality**: "g = g' iff they yield the same large-subobject predicate."
    Strictly stronger than `classify_unique`; requires the full bijection `Sub(A) ≅ Hom(A, Ω)`
    connected to `HasSubobjectClassifier.classify`. Without surjectivity of classify this is
    unavailable.

  The statement is faithful to §1.919 and stays as-is with the sorry.

**`minimal_topos_has_terminator`** (§1.91(10)) — INTEGRITY FIX. The prior version had hypotheses
`[HasSubobjectClassifier 𝒞]` + `_hne : 𝒞` and was discharged by `⟨HasSubobjectClassifier.toHasTerminal⟩`:
since `HasSubobjectClassifier extends HasTerminal`, the conclusion was FREE and `[HasBinaryProducts]`,
`[HasEqualizers]`, `_hne` were structurally unused — it did not reproduce Freyd's construction at all.

Restated faithfully with hypotheses that are EXACTLY Freyd's data and do NOT bundle a terminator:
`[HasPullbacks] [HasBinaryProducts] [HasEqualizers] [∀ C, HasPowerObject C]` and a witnessing object
`B : 𝒞`. `HasPowerObject` does not `extend HasTerminal`, so the conclusion `Nonempty (HasTerminal 𝒞)`
is now genuine. The construction (in the docstring): `T := equalizer(id_{[B]}, Λ(M_{B,B}))` where
`Λ(M_{−,B})` is constant by `f(M_{A,B}) = M_{A',B}`, making `T` terminal via equalizer factorization.

Faithful `sorry`: the constancy lemma `f(M_{A,B}) = M_{A',B}` plus the equalizer factorization are not
yet derivable from the bare Λ/∈ classify-bijection of `HasPowerObject` (no naturality of `mem` packaged).
