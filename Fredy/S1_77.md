## Summary of sorries

ALL 3 original sorry sites discharged. Zero `sorry` tokens remain in `Fredy/S1_77.lean`.

1. `equivClos_from_symm_transRefClos` (§1.775) — DONE.
   - Symmetry of Rsym*: proved via minimality of Rsym* applied to (Rsym*)°. (Rsym*)° is
     reflexive (`graph(id) ⊑ (graph id)° ⊑ (Rsym*)°`), transitive (`comp_reciprocal_le` +
     reciprocal-monotone on `hr.trans`), and ⊇ Rsym (`Rsym° ⊑ Rsym ⊑ Rsym*`, reciprocate),
     giving `Rsym* ⊑ (Rsym*)°`; reciprocate + involution to flip to `(Rsym*)° ⊑ Rsym*`.
   - Minimality `Rsym ⊑ E`: the original signature was genuinely too weak — `Rsym` abstract
     with only `R ⊑ Rsym` + symmetry cannot force `Rsym ⊑ E`. Added a faithful `hJoin`
     hypothesis (Rsym is the join of R, R°, 1, i.e. its universal property as the
     symmetrisation). With `hJoin` the proof is immediate from `hRE`, E-symmetry, E-reflexivity.
   - Caveat: transitively depends on `sorryAx` only through the cited §1.561 lemma
     `comp_reciprocal_le` (sorried upstream in S1_56, out of scope). No sorry in this file.

2. `quotClos_is_transitive` + `quotClos_eq_transRefClos` (§1.787) — DONE, fully sorry-free
   (axioms = {Classical.choice}, no sorryAx).
   ROOT CAUSE of the old blocker: the previous attempt used R/R (`RelQuot R R`), which forces
   the step `R·(R/R)·R ⊑ R·R` and then stalls needing `R·R ⊑ R` (R not transitive). The book's
   §1.787 FIRST half avoids R/R entirely: it uses the SELF-quotient S/S with S = R̄. Then
   `R ⊑ S/S` is free from `R·S ⊑ S` (= qBar.stable), S/S is reflexive+transitive (§1.786),
   so `R* ⊑ S/S` by R*-minimality, whence `R* ⊑ R̄`. Combined with `R̄ ⊑ R*` this gives R̄ = R*,
   and transitivity of R̄ follows from transitivity of R*.
   Signature change: replaced parameter `qRR : RelQuot R R` with `hr : TransRefClos R` and
   `qSS : RelQuot R̄ R̄` (the self-quotient; exists in a logos by §1.784). This is faithful —
   the theorem statement is about R̄ given that R* exists, exactly the book's setting.
