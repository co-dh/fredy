## Why the sorries — the single shared blocker (§1.472 representation argument)

Freyd proves the whole §1.471–§1.474 family through ONE device: §1.472's reduction of
specialness to *preservation of properness of subobjects by a Cartesian representation*
(`T : A → Set`, §1.437/§1.646). Every counterexample to a universal Cartesian sentence is
pushed through such a `T` to a Set counterexample. None of the eight statements below is a
direct unfolding; each routes through this machinery, which is itself only **partially
formalized** in this repo: `S1_43.lean` still has open `sorry`s in exactly that
representation layer (`pullbacks_terminal_implies_cartesianFunctor` :485/:496,
`reflects_equalizers_reflects_isos` :526). So the §1.47 layer cannot be discharged honestly
before §1.437/§1.646 is completed. Fabricating these (e.g. `:= trivial`, vacuous restatements)
would violate the project's integrity rule, so the faithful `sorry`s are kept.

### Concrete diagnostics performed this pass

- `prodEndo.map f = pair fst (snd ≫ f)`. Composing `map f = map g` with `snd` gives, by
  `snd_pair`, `snd ≫ f = snd ≫ g`. Hence the clean reformulation
  **`Embedding (prodEndo B) ↔ ∀ Y, Epi (snd : prod B Y → Y)`** (snd is the second projection
  out of `prod B Y`). This is a true, self-contained lemma but is NOT one of the existing
  sorry statements and does not by itself discharge any of them.
- The RHS of `prodEndo_faithful_iff_product_proper` is `Mono (pair (fst ≫ m) snd)`, i.e.
  `m × id_B` monic for every proper mono `m`. `m × id` is **not** monic from `m` monic alone
  (that failure is precisely what specialness rules out), and it is unrelated to `snd` being
  epic; so neither direction of the iff follows by monic/epic cancellation. The bridge is
  exactly §1.472's representation content. Confirmed both `→` and `←` branches stall on it.
- Factor-order mismatch: `special` uses `prod A' B` (B on the right) while `prodEndo B X =
  prod B X` (B on the left). The repo has **no** product-swap iso (`pair snd fst`), so even the
  bookkeeping symmetry needed to line the two sides up is absent. Adding a swap iso would only
  remove the bookkeeping gap, not the representation gap.
