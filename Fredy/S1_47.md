## Why the sorries

- **`special_atMostTwoValues`** (§1.471): Requires transferring a Set-level argument
  (subobjects of 1 in Set are singletons and empty) into the abstract special category
  via the definition of specialness. The book argument is: in Set either V₁ ↪ V₂ or V₂ ↪ 1
  is iso, so both equal V₁ ∩ V₂; transfer this universally-quantified sentence. Full
  formalization needs Set-level reasoning about subobjects of 1 not yet in this repo.

- **`prodFunctor_faithful_iff_product_proper`** (§1.472): A standard equivalence between
  injectivity of `B × -` on morphisms and properness of the product map `pair(fst≫m, snd)`.
  Requires characterizing the functor map action and using monic-cancellation.

- **`special_iff_prodFunctor_faithful`** (§1.472): The main equivalence of §1.472. The
  (⇐) direction (faithful ⇒ special) follows directly. The (⇒) direction needs
  `prodFunctor_faithful_iff_product_proper` plus induction on finite sequences.

- **`prodFunctor_faithful_all_implies_special`** (§1.473 ⇐): Consequence of
  `special_iff_prodFunctor_faithful`; needs that result proved first.

- **`oneValued_special_prodFunctor_faithful`** (§1.473 ⇒): Book argument (§1.473):
  if B ≇ 1, diagonal (id,id): B→B×B is proper, so (B×B)×- is faithful, forcing B×- faithful.
  Needs: faithfulness composition + diagonals of non-units are proper in one-valued categories.

- **`twoValued_special_prodFunctor_faithful`** / **`twoValued_special_iff`** (§1.474):
  Book argument: B×0 ≅ 0 for all B (universal property of 0); B×0 ↪ B×1 ≅ B is a proper
  subobject of B when B ≇ 0. Needs: zero-object product isomorphism not yet established.
