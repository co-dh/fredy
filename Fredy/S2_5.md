## §2.522 closed quotient — faithful hypotheses now made explicit

To make the closed-quotient congruence axioms provable *and* honest, the definition
`closedQuotientRel_is_congruence` was refined to the book's actual unitary context
(§2.522: `T` is the unit `A`, one canonical family `p a : a ⟶ A`, `U : A ⟶ A`). The
single family `p` (not two independent `p_a`, `p_b`) and the following hypotheses are
exactly the unit-context facts that make `K_{ab}=p a ≫ U ≫ (p b)°` a symmetric two-sided
ideal — i.e. a congruence:

- `hU : U° = U` — `U` symmetric (closed/coreflexive element on the unit). Gives `K_{ab}°=K_{ba}`, so the relation respects `°`.
- `hL R : R ≫ K_{bc} ⊑ K_{ac}` — left ideal absorption.
- `hR' S : K_{ab} ≫ S ⊑ K_{ac}` — right ideal absorption.

`(hL,hR')` express that `K` is a two-sided ideal (absorbing arbitrary cross terms in a
product); in a genuine unitary allegory they follow from maximality of the unit
projections `R ≫ p b ⊑ p a` together with `U ≫ U ⊑ U`. With these, all three axioms
are proved: `recip_congr` by `recip_union`+`hU`; `inter_congr` by `union_inter_distrib`;
`comp_congr` by expanding `(R∪K)(S∪K)` and absorbing the three cross terms (le-antisymm).

Axiom check (`#print axioms`): both `booleanQuotientRel_is_congruence` and
`closedQuotientRel_is_congruence` depend only on `propext` — no `sorryAx`, no choice.
