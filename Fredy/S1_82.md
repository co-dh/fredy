## Sorries and Why

1. **`complete_hasEqualizers`** (line ~181): Extracting equalizers as limits of the walking
   parallel pair (WPP) requires lifting `WPP : Type 0` to `Type v` (universe of `Complete`).
   The `ULift` approach adds boilerplate for both objects and hom-types; mathematically trivial.

2. **`general_adjoint_functor_theorem`** (line ~368): Full proof requires representability
   theory for set-valued functors (§1.834), category of elements, and splitting idempotents.
   Stated faithfully; proof is corollary of More General AFT.

3. **`special_adjoint_functor_theorem`** (line ~413): Requires cardinality argument and
   well-powered + cogenerating construction (§1.839). Deep; stated faithfully.

4. **`preserves_weaklim_iff_preserves_lim` (uniqueness)** (line ~485): The existence of a
   lift is proved. Uniqueness requires T to preserve the collectively-monic property of limit
   projections, which needs more structure than just weak-limit preservation.

5. **`more_general_adjoint_functor_theorem`** (line ~537): Full proof requires: (a) category
   of elements construction, (b) splitting idempotents, (c) §1.834-1.835 machinery.

6. **`complete_cocomplete_iff_precocomplete`** (line ~556): Depends on More General AFT
   (§1.837 uses it); sorry propagated from above.

## Key Theorems Proved

- **`complete_iff_eq_prod` (⇐)**: Given `HasEqualizers ℬ` and `HasProducts ℬ`, constructs
  `Complete ℬ` via the standard construction: lim D = eq(f, g) where
  f, g : ΠᵢDᵢ → Π_{x:i→j}Dⱼ encode the naturality condition (§1.825).

- **`limit_cone_unique`**: Any two limit cones over the same diagram are isomorphic;
  the mediating maps are mutual inverses proved by limit uniqueness (§1.822).

- **`uniformly_continuous_preserves_prelimits`**: A uniformly continuous functor sends
  any cone in 𝒜 to one factoring through a member of a cofinal family (§1.831).
