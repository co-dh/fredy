## Sorries: justification summary

All sorries correspond to the book's proofs which require one or more of:
- **Full topos machinery not yet built**: power-object ↔ coproduct constructions,
  image factorization in the power-object language (§1.951, §1.952, §1.954, §1.955).
- **Representation theorems**: §1.965 uses faithful embedding into a capital topos
  (§1.635), which is not yet in the repo.
- **Exponential/adjunction infrastructure**: §1.962, §1.966 use curry/uncurry
  naturality in ways not yet encoded.
- **Complete ↔ cocomplete**: §1.967, §1.968 are equivalent-conditions theorems
  whose proof is a multi-step chain through power/copower constructions; each step
  is non-trivial and relies on the above missing pieces.

## Sharpened blocker for the §1.967/§1.968 power↔copower targets

The task singled out `topos_powers_copowers_equiv` (a↔b),
`topos_copowers_equiv_copowers_of_one` (b↔c), and `topos_complete_iff_cocomplete`
(§1.968) as candidates that "may follow from the power/copower universal properties
plus the topos exponential adjunction." After investigation they remain genuinely
blocked, not by missing UP plumbing but by two concrete upstream gaps:

1. **`Fredy/S1_92.lean:33 topos_has_exponentials` is itself `sorry`.** `Topos`
   (Fredy/S1_9.lean:144) extends only `HasTerminal`, `HasBinaryProducts`,
   `HasSubobjectClassifier` — it does NOT carry a `HasExponentials` instance, and
   the one that would supply it (`topos_has_exponentials`) is unproven (it needs the
   §1.859 `baseable_inclusion_preserves_equilizers` fact, also `sorry`). So the
   "exponential adjunction" the hint relies on (power of A = copower transposed) is
   not available as usable `curry`/`eval` data inside these theorems.

2. **No distributivity / complemented-injection structure.** Even granting
   exponentials, both directions need a *construction*, not just a UP rearrangement:
   - (a)→(b) builds `∐ᵢ A` as a subobject of `∏ᵢ (A+1)` via complemented injections
     `uᵢuᵢ°=1, uᵢuⱼ°=0` — this needs §1.952 positivity (`topos_is_positive`, sorry)
     and image factorization.
   - (c)→(b) needs `∐ᵢ A ≅ (∐ᵢ 1) × A`, i.e. products distribute over the I-indexed
     coproduct — there is no distributivity lemma in scope, and the hypotheses give
     only bare `HasBinaryProducts`/`HasBinaryCoproducts`, not `HasAllCoproducts`.
   Each iff needs BOTH directions, so neither the trivial (b)→(c) specialisation nor
   any single UP step suffices; faking the iff with one half would be dishonest.

3. **§1.965 `cogenerator_internally_cogenerates` / `omega_internally_cogenerates`.**
   The faithful-cogenerator argument (T(C^f) ≠ T(C^g) for points T = (one,−))
   requires the unitor `prod one B ≅ B` and the transpose-of-points natural bijection
   `(one, C^^B) ≅ (B, C)`. Neither the unitor nor that naturality is in the repo
   (S1_85 has no `prod one` iso, no point-naming infrastructure). Building them is a
   separate infrastructure task, out of scope for this file.

`Iff.rfl` (definitional — both sides are the same `IsGeneratingSet` predicate).
