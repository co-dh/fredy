## Notes on sorries

### `combined_reflects_leftInv` (line ~207)
F(x) = (covCayleyMap x, P(contraCayleyMap x)) having a left inverse l implies x has a left
inverse.  The covariant C component gives `l₁(⟨A, x⟩) = ⟨A, id_A⟩` but this encodes only
A→A, not B→A.  The P(C°) component carries the key information: the predicate
`T = {s ∈ C°(B) | C°(x)(s) = ⟨A,id_A⟩}` identifies a right-inverse via l.
Full formalisation pending — the set-theoretic witness extraction requires careful
unfolding of the sigma/product structure.

### `proset_faithful_iff_injective` forward direction (line ~286)
"Faithful ⟹ injective on objects" is FALSE with our definition `Faithful = Embedding +
reflects-iso` without fullness.  Counterexample: the constant functor from the 2-element
discrete preorder {a,b} to the 1-element preorder {x} is an Embedding (no non-trivial
parallel morphisms) and reflects isos (vacuously), yet is not injective on objects.
The fully-provable version is split into `proset_inj_faithful` (←) and
`proset_full_faithful_inj` (→, requires fullness).
