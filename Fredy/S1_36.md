## How the two former sorries were discharged

### `quotientByKernel_exists` (§1.366) — DONE
The quotient `𝒟 = 𝒞/K` glues `K`-connected objects: `|𝒟| = Quotient` of `|𝒞|` by
`R X Y := ∃ f : X ⟶ Y, K.mem f`.

1. **`R` is an equivalence relation** once `EquivalenceKernel` carries the
   composition-closure field. We **added `mem_comp : K.mem f → K.mem g → K.mem (f≫g)`**
   to `EquivalenceKernel` (book-faithful — a kernel is a subcategory, `Q(fg)=1·1=1`) and
   re-proved it inside `equivalenceKernel`. Then `R` is reflexive (`mem_id`), symmetric
   (`isGroupoid`), transitive (`mem_comp`).
2. **Morphisms via a representative skeleton, not double cosets.** Classical choice picks
   a representative object `rep d` per class and a unique `K`-iso `κ X : X ⟶ rep⟦X⟧`
   (`kappa`/`kappaInv`, groupoid-inverse). The quotient category `catObj` sets
   `Hom d e := rep d ⟶ rep e` (homs of `𝒞` between representatives), so identity/comp/
   axioms are inherited verbatim. `Q := Quotient.mk`; on maps `Q f := (κX)⁻¹ ≫ f ≫ κY`.
   This makes `Q` **faithful and full on the nose** and **onto by construction** — far
   simpler than the double-coset calculus, and the unique `K`-isos play the role `KxK`
   plays in the book. The kernel clause `f∈K ↔ (QX=QY ∧ Q f ≅ 1)` follows because
   `Q f` is a `K`-map iff `f` is (`Qmap_mem_of_mem`/`mem_of_Qmap_mem`), and over equal
   endpoints `isPreorder` identifies a `K`-map with the identity.
   Axioms: `propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound` (choice is permitted — Freyd reasons
   classically and representative selection is the unavoidable AC step).

### `equivalenceFunctor_factorization` (§1.361) — DONE (axiom-free)
The original Lean statement (`Φ` a **cat-iso** with `I.T(Φ X)=F X`) was an
**over-constraint and is false in general**: a bijective `Φ` makes `I.objSet ≅ 𝒞`, so
`I.T` onto `𝒟` would force `F` onto on objects, which an equivalence functor need not be
(e.g. `F : (one object) → (two isomorphic objects)`).

We **restated it faithfully to the book**: the inflation `[T']` has objects
`(B, ∃A θ:FA≅B)` (`FactObj`, the iso recorded as `Nonempty`-style existence so the object
type stays in `Type u`), `T' := Subtype.val` (onto `𝒟` exactly by
`HasRepresentativeImage`), and `Φ X := (F X, ⟨X, 1, iso⟩)` is the book's *cross-section
followed by the iso* `[T]≅[T']`, i.e. a **full embedding** (`Embedding Φ ∧ Full Φ`), with
`T' (Φ X) = F X`. The inflation tag `B : 𝒟` is taken as input (it is a phantom label in
`Inflation B`, and `∃ B:𝒟` would otherwise fail when `𝒟` is empty). No choice is needed:
`isOnto` is an existential and `Φ`'s full-faithfulness comes directly from `F`. Verified
**zero axioms**.
