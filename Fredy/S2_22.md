# §2.228 — Union allegories (`Fredy/S2_22.lean`)

Freyd & Scedrov, *Categories, Allegories*, §2.228 (the final remark of §2.2, "Distributive
allegories").

## What the book says

Freyd considers **allegories with finite unions which distribute with composition, but not
necessarily with intersection**. A *distributive* allegory (§2.21) is exactly such a structure
that *also* satisfies the intersection-over-union law

```
R ∩ (S ∪ T) = (R ∩ S) ∪ (R ∩ T).
```

He then records three observations:

| # | statement | proof idea (book) |
|---|-----------|-------------------|
| (a) | A **tabular** allegory with this property is distributive. | Tabulate `R ∪ S ∪ T` by `(f,g)`; the poset `{Q ⊑ f°g}` is isomorphic to the coreflexives of `□f`, which form a distributive lattice (∩ = composition on coreflexives). |
| (b) | A **semi-simple** allegory with this property is distributive. | Split the symmetric idempotents to get a tabular allegory with the same property, then apply (a). |
| (c) | An allegory with this property in which **every morphism is the union of the semi-simple morphisms it contains** [2.225] **need not** be distributive. | Witness: for a group `G` with `|G| > 2`, adjoin a zero `0` and a maximal element `M` to the one-object allegory on `G` (`G` discretely ordered, `RM = MR = M` for `R ≠ 0`, `0` absorbing, reciprocation = inverse fixing `0`,`M`). |

The point of (c) is that the union-of-semi-simples hypothesis alone is *weaker* than tabularity
or semi-simplicity: it does **not** force distributivity.

## What is formalized

- `UnionAllegory 𝒜` — the §2.228 structure: the `DistributiveAllegory` data/laws of §2.21
  **minus** the single axiom `inter_union_distrib`. It keeps zero, union (commutative,
  idempotent, associative, zero-unit, absorption with ∩), zero absorbing under composition, and
  **composition distributing over union**. The omitted ∩-over-∪ law is documented in a comment
  inside the class.
- `instance distributiveAllegory_isUnionAllegory` — every `DistributiveAllegory` (§2.21) is a
  `UnionAllegory`. (Proved; establishes the structure is genuinely weaker, not vacuous.)
- `InterUnionDistrib 𝒜` — the predicate "the omitted law holds"; a union allegory is
  distributive iff this holds.
- `UnionOfSemiSimpleUA R` — "`R` is the union of the semi-simple morphisms it contains" (§2.225),
  as a finite list of semi-simple sub-morphisms whose union is `R`.

### Order theory for union allegories (fully proved, reused below)

The lattice axioms shared with §2.21 (the absorption laws + union semigroup laws) already
recover the order theory of `∪` — **none** of these proofs needs the absent `inter_union_distrib`:

- `le_iff_unionU_eq_left`, `unionU_lub`, `le_unionU_left/right`, `unionU_mono`.
- `interUnionU_distrib_ge` — the always-free **easy half** `(R∩S) ∪ (R∩T) ⊑ R ∩ (S∪T)`.
- `interUnionDistrib_iff_le` — `InterUnionDistrib` reduces to the **reverse** containment
  `R ∩ (S∪T) ⊑ (R∩S) ∪ (R∩T)`; the easy half is for free.

### The §2.228(c) counterexample (fully constructive)

Built explicitly as the one-object allegory `Q3 = {𝟎, e, a, a², M}` (`G = ℤ/3`, |G| = 3 > 2):

- `Q3.meet`/`Q3.join` (discrete lattice on `G`, `𝟎` bottom, `M` top), `Q3.comp` (group product,
  `𝟎` absorbing, `M` maximal), `Q3.recip` (inverse, `a° = a²`).
- `Cat Unit`, `counterAllegory : Allegory Unit`, `counterUnionAllegory : UnionAllegory Unit` —
  **every** allegory and union-allegory axiom discharged by finite case analysis (`cases … <;> rfl`).
- `Q3.simple_of` — the simples are exactly `{𝟎, e, a, a²}` (not `M`); `Q3.semiSimple_of`,
  `counter_unionOfSemiSimple` — every morphism is a union of semi-simples it contains
  (non-`M` ones are themselves semi-simple; `M = e ∪ a`).
- Non-distributivity at `R = a, S = e, T = a²`: `a ∩ (e ∪ a²) = a ∩ M = a`, but
  `(a∩e) ∪ (a∩a²) = 𝟎 ∪ 𝟎 = 𝟎`.

`#print axioms` confirms `exists_unionOfSemiSimple_not_distributive` (and all the (c) lemmas,
the iff-reduction, `tab_phi_coreflexive`) depend on **`propext` only** — no `Classical.choice`,
no `sorryAx`.

Each remaining `sorry` sits inside the book's real mathematical statement. None is `: True`, a
vacuous `trivial`, an unused-hypothesis stub, or an over-promising name. The hypotheses in (a),(b)
(`Tabular`/`SemiSimple` everywhere) are genuinely consumed: (a) extracts the tabulation and the
`f°f=g°g=1` identities and the coreflexive `φ`-images; (b) feeds the semi-simple witnesses to its
gap. The two gaps are sharply-stated, single-purpose lemmas.
