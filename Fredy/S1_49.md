## Notes on remaining sorries

The three subterminator theorems require showing that if `T` is a subterminator and
`f : T → T'` is an isomorphism, then `T' = T` and `f = Cat.id T`. This requires the
category to be "skeletal" (isomorphic objects are equal), which is not a τ-category axiom.
The book may intend this only for concrete τ-categories (e.g., the free one), or may
work in a setting where objects are identified up to isomorphism. These are left as honest
sorries pending further analysis of the intended setting.
