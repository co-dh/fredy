### `pasting_lemma` (§1.62) — DONE (this session)

Proven exactly per Freyd p.101. Carries `[HasBinaryCoproducts 𝒞]` in addition to `[PreLogos 𝒞]`
(a *positive* pre-logos, §1.623): the relational-union/distributivity infrastructure of §1.616
(`relUnion ∪ᵣ`, `compose_union_right`) is, in this repo, only built under `[HasBinaryCoproducts 𝒞]`,
and every downstream consumer (§1.624, §1.631) already works in a positive pre-logos.

Construction: the pushout cocone is `pt := (HasSubobjectUnions.union A₁ A₂).dom = U.dom`, with
`ι₁ = x`, `ι₂ = y` the two union inclusions (`x ≫ U.arr = A₁.arr`, `y ≫ U.arr = A₂.arr`). For an
arbitrary cocone `(Q, f, g)`, the descent morphism is extracted from the relation
`R = (graph x)°⊚graph f ∪ᵣ (graph y)°⊚graph g`, shown **Entire** (`1_U ≤ R⊚R°`, via the joint cover
`case x y`) and **Simple** (`R°⊚R ≤ 1_Q`, via four atomic terms — the one cross-term uses the cocone
compatibility through the intersection relation), hence a **Map** (`tabulated_is_map_iff_left_iso`);
`map_to_graph` yields `q` with `graph q ≃ R`. The factorizations `x ≫ q = f`, `y ≫ q = g` follow
purely from `graph x` being entire + `graph_faithful` (NO compatibility needed for fac — that was the
unlock). Uniqueness is joint-epi: `case x y` is a cover (`union_case_cover`), hence epic.

Uses the proven `compose_union_right` (S1_60), which itself transitively carries the isolated
extensivity `sorryAx` in `union_compose_descent` (S1_60) — so `pasting_lemma`'s axiom set is
`[propext, Classical.choice, sorryAx]`. No sorry of its own.

New helpers added to S1_62 (all real, sorry-free of their own): `map_to_graph`,
`cover_of_image_factor`, `union_is_image`, `union_case_cover`, `pairxx_factor`, `jcc_le`,
`union_joint_cover`, `graph_comp_recip_le_one_of_mono`, `inter_lemma`, `hxyg_lemma`, `diag_le_one`,

### `decompose_via_coproduct` (§1.624) — STILL SORRY, blocked on §1.621 infra

`Isomorphic A (coprod A₁ A₂)` with `A₁ = f#(inl).dom`, `A₂ = f#(inr).dom`. The pasting-lemma route
(avoiding the S1_61 `disjoint_cover_is_coproduct` sorry) needs two pieces that are **not in S1_62's
import closure**:
1. **`InverseImage` preserves intersection** — to get `A₁∩A₂ ≤ f#(inl∩inr) ≤ f#(⊥) ≅ ⊥`. S1_60 has
   only `invImage_preserves_union` and `invImage_preserves_bottom`, not inter.
2. **pushout-over-initial = coproduct** — `pasting_lemma` gives `HasPushout` of the intersection legs;
   with the intersection initial this *is* the coproduct, but the construction
   `coprod_is_pushout_of_init` is `private` in `S1_65` (downstream — circular).
Plus the §1.621 disjointness hypotheses (`inl∩inr≤0`, `inl∪inr=⊤`) would have to be threaded in.
Provable in principle once (1)+(2) are built once; that is a multi-lemma infrastructure task larger
than a single-file fill, and the needed pieces sit downstream of S1_62.

### `complemented_of_projective_is_projective` (§1.631) — STILL SORRY (1 hole), blocked on coproduct disjointness

Proof skeleton complete (incl. `Cover h` via `union_via_coproduct_image`). Remaining hole (the
`obtain ⟨r, hr⟩ : ∃ r : P ⟶ B, r ≫ y = id_P`): the clean route shows `σ : P → B+P'` factors as
`σ = r ≫ inl_B`, then `r ≫ y = id_P` from `h_inl : inl_B ≫ h = y ≫ inl_P` + `inl_P` monic. The
factoring "σ goes through `inl_B`" is precisely the §1.621 disjoint-coproduct decomposition (because
`σ ≫ h = inl_P` lands in the inl-summand, disjoint from `inr_{P'}`). That decomposition needs
`inl`,`inr` jointly monic / `inl∩inr=0`, which `[PositivePreLogos]` does not give and which lives in
the `DisjointBinaryCoproduct` class of **S1_64 (downstream — circular)**. Adding the disjointness
facts as explicit hypotheses is faithful, but consuming them still requires an
`InverseImage`-of-cocone / disjoint-factoring lemma not present in S1_62's closure. Provable in
principle; not a short fill.

Note: the theorem's `(x : A ⟶ P) (hx : Cover x)` hypotheses appear unused by the book proof
(possible statement defect) — left as-is, outside the edit scope of this pass.
