## Blocker analysis (verified 2026-06-16, two independent sub-agent probes + manual)

Both sorries are the genuine distributivity content of §1.616 (Freyd: "Direct images always
preserve unions").  They are NOT one-liners; each is blocked on infrastructure that does not yet
exist in the repo.  Neither weakening nor faking is acceptable, so the faithful `sorry`s remain.

### `compose_union_right` — the self-contained cover route is blocked on coproduct extensivity

I attempted the same cover⊥mono technique that proves `relUnion_le_reciprocal`.  Full
reconstruction (all steps below typecheck green; only the last fails):

1. `mST = case (pair S.colA S.colB)(pair T.colA T.colB)`; `image.lift mST : coprod S.src T.src →
   (S∪ᵣT).src` is a `Cover` (`image_lift_cover`).
2. `pbST = pullback(R.colB, (S∪ᵣT).colA)`; pull the cover `image.lift mST` back along `pbST.π₂`
   (needs `[PullbacksTransferCovers 𝒞]` via `cover_pullback`) → `Q` with cover leg `q₂ : Q → pbST.pt`
   and `q₁ : Q → coprod S.src T.src`.
3. Pull `q₁` back along `inl`/`inr` → `Q_S, Q_T` with legs `s₁ : Q_S → Q`, `t₁ : Q_T → Q`.
4. Build `dS : Q_S → U.src`, `dT : Q_T → U.src` (U = (R⊚S)∪ᵣ(R⊚T), pU = pair U.colA U.colB monic)
   with `dS ≫ pU = s₁ ≫ q₂ ≫ spanST` and the symmetric `dT`.  Assemble `cmp = case s₁ t₁` and
   `dJoin = case dS dT`; prove `dJoin ≫ pU = cmp ≫ (q₂ ≫ spanST)` by `case_uniq`.  ALL GREEN.
5. **BLOCKED:** to descend `dJoin` to a single `d : Q → U.src` via `cover_mono_diagonal` one needs
   `Cover cmp`, i.e. `Cover (case s₁ t₁)` — exactly that the two pullbacks of `q₁` along the
   coproduct injections JOINTLY COVER `Q`.  That is **coproduct extensivity** (universal + disjoint
   coproducts), strictly stronger than `RegularCategory`/`PullbacksTransferCovers`.

The repo has no extensivity / `Cover (case _ _)` lemma.  `disjoint_cover_is_coproduct`
(S1_61.lean:298) is the CONVERSE (disjoint cover ⟹ coproduct), not coproduct ⟹ joint cover.
`relUnion_le_reciprocal` escapes this because reciprocation is an iso (`pair snd fst`), so the
copairing transports directly with no splitting; composition is not an iso, so it does not.

### Both sorries — the book's route needs a BinRel↔Subobject(A×B) bridge that is missing

Freyd derives §1.616 from `PreLogos.invImage_preserves_union` via two identities:
  `yS = (y×1)#(S)` (precompose by a graph = inverse image along `y×1`) and
  `x∘(yS) = (x×1)(yS)` (postcompose = DIRECT image along `x×1`).
But `relUnion`/`intersect`/`compose` are defined at the **BinRel** level (image of a copairing /
pullback of pairings / image of a span in A×B), while `PreLogos.invImage_preserves_union` is stated
for the **abstract** `HasSubobjectUnions.union` field on `Subobject (prod A B)`.  These two unions
are never identified.  Missing pieces (confirmed absent by grep):
  - no `BinRel 𝒞 A B → Subobject 𝒞 (prod A B)` conversion (the monic `pair R.colA R.colB` is used
    ad hoc but never packaged), nor the inverse;
  - no theorem `relUnion R S ≅ HasSubobjectUnions.union ⟨pairR⟩ ⟨pairS⟩`;
  - no `yS = (y×1)#S` (only `graphMono_prodMap`, S1_92.lean:139, a fragment);
  - no `x∘(yS) = (x×1)(yS)` (composition-as-direct-image) at all.

### What would unblock (precise, for a follow-up)

Add to S1_56/S1_51 (NOT S1_60, which is import-frozen here): (a) `relSub : BinRel 𝒞 A B →
Subobject 𝒞 (prod A B)` and its lattice-iso to `relUnion`/`intersect`; (b) `compose_eq_directImage`:

## SHARPENED blocker (verified 2026-06-16, this agent — third probe, deeper than above)

Three additional facts were established by exhaustively trying the "direct images preserve unions"
route the task recipe favored (the route that is supposed to avoid extensivity).  All three reduce
the two remaining `sorry`s to facts that are **provably absent** at this import frontier, not merely
unwritten.  Faithful `sorry`s therefore remain; **no fake, no vacuous `True`, no `axiom`.**

### (1) The BOOK itself hypothesizes `pre-logos`; the current `compose_union_right` is UNDER-hypothesized.
`categories-allegories.txt` line 6055: "**In a pre-logos** B&(A,B) is a distributive lattice."
The whole §1.616 derivation (`yS=(y×1)#S`, "inverse images preserve unions", `x∘(yS)=(x×1)(yS)`,
"direct images always preserve unions") lives inside a pre-logos.  In a bare regular category
`compose_union_right` is **false** (composition need not distribute over union without the
pre-logos axiom).  So the faithful statement needs `[PreLogos 𝒞]` — but see (3): even WITH it the
axiom as currently stated is too weak.

### (2) Every honest sub-route for `compose_union_right` collapses to the SAME single-map split.
`R ⊚ U = image(spanU)`, `spanU : pbU.pt → A×C`, `pbU = pullback(R.colB, U.colA)`.  I verified that
each of the three standard reductions terminates at "find ONE map `pbST.pt → ((R⊚S)∪(R⊚T)).src`
agreeing with `spanST`":
  - `image_min`/`Allows` directly → needs `Allows V spanST` (one map);
  - `image_cover_comp` (S1_56:1440, `image(c≫f)=image f` for `c` cover) → needs a cover
    `e : coprod pbS.pt pbT.pt ↠ pbST.pt` so that `e≫spanST` factors per-summand;
  - `relLe_of_cover_factor` (S1_56:1490) with cover `image.lift spanST : pbST.pt ↠ (R⊚(S∪T)).src`
    → needs `φ : pbST.pt → V.src` with `φ ≫ pair V.colA V.colB = spanST` (one map again).
The single map exists iff `pbST.pt` splits as `pbS.pt ⊔ pbT.pt` over `coprod S.src T.src`, i.e. iff
pullback along `pbST.π₂` preserves the coproduct `image.lift mST : coprod S.src T.src ↠ (S∪T).src`.
That is **coproduct extensivity** (universal+disjoint coproducts), strictly stronger than
`PullbacksTransferCovers`/`RegularCategory`.  `image.lift mST` being a cover and `cover_pullback`
(regularity) give a cover `Q ↠ pbST.pt`, but `Q = pullback(case…, π₂)` does NOT decompose over the
two injections without extensivity.  So the "direct image preserves joins" content is **trivial at
the raw `image` level** (`case h k ≫ g = case (h≫g)(k≫g)` by `case_uniq`, so
`image(case h k ≫ g)` IS the copairing-union of `image(h≫g)`, `image(k≫g)`) — the ENTIRE
difficulty is `compose_eq_directImage`, and THAT step is where extensivity (or the U-independent
fixed-projection reformulation of `compose`) is unavoidable.  The repo's `compose` uses the
U-DEPENDENT pullback `pullback(R.colB, U.colA)`; the book's route uses the U-INDEPENDENT projection
`π_{BC} : A×B×C → B×C`.  Proving repo-`compose` = projection-`compose` needs a ternary product
`A×B×C`, three projections, `prodMap`, and `directImage`/`InverseImage`-of-relation — **none of
which exist** (grep for `prod (prod`, `prodMap`, `directImage`, `ternary` in S1_45/51/56 → empty).

### (3) Even granting `[PreLogos]`, the axiom `invImage_preserves_union` is TOO WEAK for the goal.
`inverseImage_preserves_unions` (S1_60:69) and `PreLogos.invImage_preserves_union` deliver only
`Isomorphic (…).dom (…).dom` — a **bare object iso** (`Isomorphic A B := ∃ f, IsIso f`, S1_34:24),
with NO commuting condition over `A`.  But `rel_inter_union_le` needs `Subobject.le`, i.e.
`∃ h, h ≫ T.arr = S.arr` (S1_51:31) — the iso must commute with the monic into `A×B`.  `intersect`
(S1_56:151) IS literally `InverseImage eR ⟨eS⟩` (pullback of the two pair-monics), so
`rel_inter_union_le` is exactly inverse-image-preserves-union in `Sub(A×B)` — but the pre-logos
axiom's bare `Isomorphic` cannot supply the commuting `h`.  **Fix:** restate
`inverseImage_preserves_unions f` as a `Subobject.le` (or full `=`) of subobjects of `A`, not an
`Isomorphic` of bare domains.  That is a one-line strengthening in S1_60's own `def`, BUT it then
makes `PreLogos` (S1_52/S1_60 `class`, import-frozen for THIS task) carry a stronger field that
every `PreLogos` instance downstream must re-prove — out of scope here, and a genuine API change.

## Structure

- `HasSubobjectUnions` — existence of joins in `Sub(B)` for each `B`
- `InverseImage` — pullback of a subobject along a morphism
- `inverseImage_preserves_unions` — predicate: `f*(S₁ ∪ S₂) = f*S₁ ∪ f*S₂`
- `PreLogos` — extends `RegularCategory` + `HasSubobjectUnions` + `invImage_preserves_union`
- `IsDistributiveLattice` / `poset_prelogos_iff_distributive` — §1.616 BinRel(A,B) is distributive iff PreLogos
- `relUnion` — image of `case(pairR, pairS) : R.src+S.src → A×B`
