## 2026-06-18 — Conceptual: what a pre-logos IS (modern terms, the logic dictionary)

Plain-English companion to the formalization above. Answers the recurring "what does this
*mean*" questions.

### Pre-logos = coherent category

"Pre-logos" is Freyd's name for what modern categorical logic calls a **coherent category**.
The hierarchy, with the first-order-logic fragment each level interprets soundly (each level is
the *minimal* category structure that interprets its fragment with substitution):

| Freyd          | Modern name       | Logic fragment           |
| -------------- | ----------------- | ------------------------ |
| regular        | regular category  | `⊤  ∧  ∃`                |
| **pre-logos**  | **coherent category** | `⊤  ∧  ∃  ⊥  ∨`      |
| logos          | Heyting category  | full first-order: `+ ⇒ ∀`|
| (＋ exact ＋ extensive coproducts) | pretopos | coherent + quotients |

A pre-logos is NOT "the thing before categorical logic" — it IS a level of categorical logic.
It adds, on top of a regular category, **finite unions of subobjects (`∨`, `⊥`) that are stable
under pullback** — exactly what is needed to interpret disjunction and falsity under substitution.

Terminology caveat: **"logos" is overloaded today.** Freyd's logos = Heyting category; Anel–Joyal
use "logos" for a Grothendieck topos viewed algebraically. Check which is meant.

### The logic ↔ subobject-lattice dictionary

A predicate on `A` = a subobject of `A`. The connectives are operations on the lattice `𝒮ub(A)`:

| Logic        | `𝒮ub(A)`         | Categorical op                          |
| ------------ | ---------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| `⊤` true     | top element      | identity `A ↣ A`                        |
| `⊥` false    | bottom element   | least subobject `0 ↣ A` (§1.61)         |
| `∧` and      | meet             | intersection = pullback of subobjects   |
| `∨` or       | join             | union = image of `S + T → A` (§1.615)   |
| `∃` exists   | —                | direct image along a map                |
| `∀` forall   | —                | universal image (Heyting only)          |

`⊤`/`⊥`: "top"/"bottom" are the names; "true"/"false" the meaning. `⊥` is an upside-down `⊤`.

### Why a regular category already has `∧` and `∃`

- `∧, ⊤` come from **finite limits** alone: intersection of `S,T ↣ A` is the pullback
  `S ↣ A ← T`; `⊤` is the identity `A ↣ A`. Even a bare Cartesian category has these.
- `∃` is what **regularity** adds. `∃_f(S)` = the **image** of `S ↣ A --f--> B` (smallest
  subobject of `B` that `S → B` factors through). In Set: `{b : ∃ a∈S, f(a)=b}` — the "∃" is
  literal. Soundness needs the two regular axioms: (1) image factorization (cover-then-mono)
  exists, so the image is definable; (2) covers are pullback-stable (Beck–Chevalley), so `∃`
  commutes with substitution.

In the repo `∃_f` is `existsAlong` (`S1_60.lean:696`): `existsAlong g U := image (U.arr ≫ g)`,
with the adjunction `∃_g ⊣ g#` as `existsAlong_le_iff` (`S1_60.lean:704`):
`(existsAlong g P).le V ↔ P.le (InverseImage g V)`. A structured packaging `DirectImage` (bundling
`directImage_adj`) is in `S1_70.lean:102`.

### `f#` (inverse image) and the adjoint string

`f#` is defined in **§1.451** by **pullback**: for `f : A → B`, the inverse image of a subobject
`B₁ ↣ B` is its pullback along `f`. Collecting over all subobjects gives the order-preserving
`f# : 𝒮ub(B) → 𝒮ub(A)` (a contravariant functor `𝒮ub(−)`; in Set it is the preimage / contravariant
power-set functor). Two facts about the definition:
- `B₁` is an **arbitrary** subobject of `B` — no relation to `im(f)`. It may be a proper part of
  `im(f)`, overlap it, or miss it (then `f#(B₁) = ⊥`). In Set `f#(B₁) = f⁻¹(B₁) = f⁻¹(B₁ ∩ im f)`,
  but the definition never requires `B₁ ⊆ im f`.
- `f#(B₁) ↣ A` is generally a **proper** subobject of `A`; it is all of `A` iff `B₁ ⊇ im f`.

`f#` sits in the middle of an adjoint string (the quantifiers are its adjoints, built from `f`):

```
        ∃_f ⊣ f# ⊣ ∀_f          (𝒮ub(A) ⇄ 𝒮ub(B))
```

- `∃_f ⊣ f#` (direct image ⊣ inverse image) — holds in any **regular** category. In Set it is the
  image/preimage Galois connection: `image(S) ⊆ T ⟺ S ⊆ preimage(T)`. This adjunction is what
  makes `∃` sound; Beck–Chevalley is its compatibility with pullback squares.
- `f# ⊣ ∀_f` (right adjoint) — needs a **Heyting** category (logos); that is why `∀` is full-FO,
  not coherent. Variance: `f#: 𝒮ub(B)→𝒮ub(A)`, both adjoints go back `𝒮ub(A)→𝒮ub(B)`.

Adjoint-side reading of the two preservation facts: `f#` is a right adjoint so it **preserves meets**
(`∧`, free — the easy half of "lattice homomorphism"); `∃_f` is a left adjoint so it **preserves
joins** (`∨`). The substantive pre-logos axiom is that `f#` *also* preserves joins — "union
preserved under inverse image" — which is NOT automatic and is the content behind the `sorry`s above.

### The three definitions of pre-logos coincide (§1.6, ports in `S1_61.lean`)

1. (full) regular + each `𝒮ub(A)` a lattice + each `f#` a **lattice homomorphism**.
2. (elementary) each pair of subobjects has a **union preserved under inverse image**.
3. (§1.611) Cartesian + images + **pullbacks transfer finite covers**.

Defs 1 ≡ 2 by construction (`PreLogos` bundles them; meet-preservation is free via S1_45
`invImg_preserves_inter`, so "lattice hom" reduces to "preserves `∨` and `⊥`"). Def 3 ⟺ Def 1/2 is
proven in `S1_61.lean` as `prelogos_iff_transfersFiniteUnions`, but **only with extra hypotheses**:
the forward inclusion `f#(S∪T) ≤ f#S ∪ f#T` is NOT derivable from a bare regular category — it
genuinely needs **coproduct extensivity / positivity (§1.623)**, which is exactly why Freyd states
"transfer finite covers" as a hypothesis. (This replaced an earlier `cartesian_with_images_covers_implies_prelogos`
that falsely claimed bare `RegularCategory ⟹ PreLogos`.) Same extensivity wall as the §1.616 `sorry`s above.

### Punchline: why "lattice hom" collapses to "union preserved", and what does NOT prove it

```
regular category:   ∩ exists, f# preserves ∩     ← FREE (limits compose: f# is itself a pullback)
pre-logos adds:     ∪ exists, f# preserves ∪     ← the actual new axiom
```

The two definitions (full "lattice homomorphism" vs. elementary "union preserved under inverse
image") match **not** because join-preservation implies meet-preservation, but because the
meet/intersection side was never in question — it comes for free in any regular (indeed any
finite-limit) category, since `f#` is itself a pullback and pullbacks compose (`f#(Y₁∩Y₂) =
f#Y₁ ∩ f#Y₂`). So everything about `∩` in "lattice hom" is automatic; the only genuinely new
content is that `∪` exists and `f#` preserves it.

**Common mistake — "preserve `∪` ⟹ preserve `∩` by reversing the arrows" is FALSE.** Meet and join
are order-dual, but a single map preserving one need not preserve the other: a **left** adjoint
preserves joins (not meets), a **right** adjoint preserves meets (not joins). Reversing arrows lands
you in `Cᵒᵖ` talking about a *different* map, not the same `f#`. The real reason `f#` preserves meets
is that `f#` is a **right adjoint** (`∃_f ⊣ f#`), and right adjoints preserve limits = meets — the
automatic half. Preserving joins is the *additional* axiom that makes a regular category a pre-logos.
