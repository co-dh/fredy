## Blockers (remaining)

- **Rel(E) as a category**: ¬ß1.842, ¬ß1.845, ¬ß1.846, ¬ß1.847 all require `Rel(E)` to have a `Cat`
  instance. `BinRel E A B` types relations between two fixed objects; the full `Rel(E)` category
  (objects = objects of E, morphisms = BinRel up to ‚Č°) is not yet formalized. This is the biggest
  missing piece and the only thing left blocking ¬ß1.845/¬ß1.846.

- **Arbitrary-union composition** (¬ß1.845/¬ß1.846): composing a relation with an arbitrary
  supremum of subobjects; needs `LocallyComplete'` (¬ß1.844, already in GrothendieckTopos) and
  the distributivity law (¬ß1.844 "the composition of relations distributes with arbitrary unions").
  Even with that, the *uniqueness* clauses of both theorems quantify over arbitrary `BinRel`
  solutions and need Rel(E)-composition identities, so they are gated on the Rel(E) category.
