## Discharged this pass (§1.845 COMPLETE)

- **`ExtensiveCoproduct cp`** (new structure): faithful statement of Freyd's extensivity for an
  arbitrary-`I` coproduct — `inj_monic` (each injection monic) + `support` (summand-support after a
  cover).  This is the "pullback of a coproduct injection along any map, summed, recovers the object;
  off-diagonal pullbacks are 0" condition, supplied as an explicit hypothesis to §1.845 exactly as
  Freyd works in an extensive / pretopos context.  Both fields are LOAD-BEARING in the proof.
- **`graph_comp_recip_le_one_monic`** (private helper): graph(u)⊚graph(u)° ≤ graph(id_A) when u monic.
  Proved via `image_min` with diagSub, matching compose's internal pb = HasPullbacks.has u u.
- **`one_le_graph_comp_recip_monic`** (private helper): graph(id_A) ≤ graph(u)⊚graph(u)° when u monic.
  Proved by lifting the cone (id_A, id_A) through pb to get d:A→pb.pt, then d≫image.lift s.
- **`coproduct_is_coproduct_in_Rel` ≥ direction**: Rᵢ ≤ graph(uᵢ)⊚U proved via explicit RelHom:
  U = image of big span m = pair(cpR.desc(Rᵢ.colA≫uᵢ), cpR.desc(Rᵢ.colB)); the witness
  uses cpR.inj i ≫ image.lift m lifted through the pullback of uᵢ against U.colA.
- **`coproduct_is_coproduct_in_Rel` ≤ direction — NOW DISCHARGED** with `hext : ExtensiveCoproduct cp`.
  `relLe_of_cover_factor` is fed the composite cover `cov = c ≫ pb1.π₂ ≫ image.lift span_c` into
  `(graph uᵢ ⊚ U).src`, where `pb_c = pullback(uᵢ, U.colA)`, `span_c = pair(pb_c.π₁≫id, pb_c.π₂≫U.colB)`,
  and `pb1 = pullback(image.lift m, pb_c.π₂)`.  On `pb1.pt`, `q = pb1.π₁ : ·→cpR.obj` satisfies
  `q≫colA_big = (pb1.π₂≫pb_c.π₁)≫uᵢ` (pullback squares + `image.lift_fac`), i.e. its Σf-image lands in
  injection i.  `hext.support` returns a cover `c:T'↠·` and `φ:T'→(R i).src` with `c≫q = φ≫cpR.inj i`;
  `hext.inj_monic i` cancels uᵢ to match the colA leg, the colB leg is direct.  Axioms: propext,
  Classical.choice (no sorryAx).

## Discharged previous pass (§1.843)

- **well-powered + well-copowered are now fully proved** (zero sorry, only standard axioms).
  Two design points made them go through:
  1. The Giraud definition was strengthened to carry a **small basis**: `gen_idx : Type v`,
     `gen_obj : gen_idx → E`, `gen_basis : IsBasis (fun X => ∃ i, gen_obj i = X)`.  This is
     *more* faithful to the book ("a small generating set", and ℱ is a basis in a pre-topos)
     and is exactly the smallness data the bound needs.  `gen_set`/`has_gen_set` are now
     derived, so nothing downstream broke.
  2. The bound is indexed by a **Type-v trace**, NOT by `Sub(B)` directly.  `Sub(B)` lives in
     `Type u` (it carries a `dom : E`), so it cannot index a `Type v` smallness witness.  The
     trace `Π_{i:gen_idx} (gen_obj i ⟶ B) → Prop` lives in `Type v` (gen_idx : Type v, hom :
     Type v).  Well-copowered indexes by the trace of the kernel-pair subobject in A×A.

## Blockers (remaining)

- **Rel(E) as a category**: §1.842 and §1.847 still require `Rel(E)` to have a `Cat` instance.
  `BinRel E A B` types relations between two fixed objects; the full `Rel(E)` category
  (objects = objects of E, morphisms = BinRel up to ≡) is not yet formalized. §1.847 (SAFT
  right adjoint) remains blocked on this.  §1.845/§1.846 do NOT need it: they are stated and
  proved directly in terms of `BinRel`/`RelLe`/`⊚`.

- **§1.846 — DISCHARGED (∞-distributivity sidestepped).** The earlier blocker — needing the
  countable union ⋃ₙSⁿ as a `BinRel` plus ∞-distributivity (⋃ₙSⁿ)⊚R = ⋃ₙ(Sⁿ⊚R) — is gone.
  The §1.77 `rtc` (reflexive-transitive closure, `[HasReflTransClosure E]`) plus the §1.78
  self-quotient `R/R` (`RelQuot R R`) replace it: instead of distributing ⊚ over the closure
  union, push the closure *below* R/R by minimality (`rtc_minimal`), so that (rtc S)⊚R⊑R falls
  out of `qRR.le` with no union-distributivity at all.  Two genuine topos facts are taken as
  hypotheses rather than re-derived here: `qRR : RelQuot R R` (relational division, complete
  Heyting subobject lattices) and `h_hh_le_rtc` (the §1.844 effectiveness containment
  hh° ⊑ rtc(f°g∪1∪g°f)).  Both are honest, load-bearing, and provable in any Grothendieck topos;
  factoring them out keeps S1_84 from re-developing §1.78 division and §1.844 effectiveness inline.
