# §1.75 — The Stone Representation Theorem (atom vocabulary)

Freyd & Scedrov, *Categories, Allegories*, §1.75–§1.755.

§1.75 proves the **Stone Representation Theorem** for logoi: every small (positive) logos has a
faithful representation `A → 𝓜(X)` into sheaves on a compact, totally-disconnected space `X`
without isolated points (Cantor space / the rationals / the irrationals in the countable case).

The bulk of the section is irreducibly **point-set topological** (Stone spaces of boolean
algebras, ultra-filters, stalk/sheaf functors, the Cantor space and the real line — Appendix A).
That machinery does not exist in this repo, which is hand-built category theory on `Cat`. So this
file formalizes only the §1.751 **vocabulary of atoms**, which opens the proof and is cleanly
stateable from existing infrastructure.

| Item (book)                          | Lean name                       | Status        |

| §1.751 ATOM (`0` is its unique proper subobject) | `IsAtom`            | def           |

| §1.751 ATOMLESS (no atoms)           | `Atomless`                      | def           |

- `IsAtom A` = `0` is proper (so `A` is not a zero-object — Freyd: "the definition of atom
  excludes zero-objects") **and** every proper subobject of `A` is `≤ 0`. Combined with
  `PreLogos.bottom_min` (`0 ≤ S` always), this pins every proper subobject to `0`, i.e. "`0` is its
  unique proper subobject" verbatim.
- `AtomicallyBased = IsBasis (fun A => IsAtom A)` reuses the §1.632 `IsBasis` (collective
  faithfulness + separation of proper subobjects). DRY: no new "basis" notion introduced.
- `atomicallyBased_isBoolean` states the genuine book theorem with its honest content —
  *every subobject is complemented* (excluded middle), which is exactly the `hasComplement` field
  of `BooleanPreLogos`. The proof requires constructing `¬S` as the indexed join of all atom-images
  not factoring through `S`. The single blocking gap is **`HasIndexedSubobjectJoins`**: this repo's
  `HasSubobjectUnions` (S1_60) provides only binary joins; no indexed/arbitrary-join typeclass exists.
  Without it the complement cannot be constructed even classically. Secondary obligations (inverse-image
  preservation of unions, the `IsComplemented` disjointness clause) unblock once the join is available.
  Honest `sorry` with precise gap named in the docstring.
- **No binary-join / induction escape (verified).** `IsBasis` (§1.632) unfolds to
  `IsGeneratingSet ℱ ∧ (∀ proper mono m, ∃ G, ℱ G ∧ ∃ x : G → A, x ∤ m)` — it yields *one* witnessing
  atom per proper subobject and **never** bounds the atom basis to finitely many atoms nor produces a
  finite cover of `A`. Hence the complement's index set `{(G,x) | G atom, x : G→A, x ∤ S}` is genuinely
  unbounded: no finite/binary fold over `HasSubobjectUnions.union` and no structural induction over the
  basis can reach it. The arbitrary-join requirement is essential, not an artifact of the encoding.

## MISSING (not emitted as Lean — would be vacuous/false)

- **§1.751 periodic power `℘A`** — periodic functions `ℕ → A`, sublogos of `A^ℕ`, with a faithful
  atomless diagonal representation `A → ℘A`. Needs countable powers, the periodic-sequence
  sublogos, faithfulness, and preservation of "positive & capital". None present.
- **§1.752 Stone space `𝒮(ℬ)`** of the boolean algebra `ℬ` of complemented subterminators
  (points = ultra-filters; basic opens `Û`). Needs ultra-filters + topological spaces.
- **§1.752–§1.753 stalk/sheaf functor** `T : A → 𝓜(X)` with its two characterizing properties
  ((1) stalks `= rℱ`; (2) image of `Sub(A) → Sub(TA)` is a basis), and the descent showing `T`
  faithful when `T : A → 𝓜(X)` is faithful. Needs sheaves `𝓜(X)` and stalk functors.
- **§1.75 / §1.754 / §1.755 Stone Representation Theorem** proper (faithful rep into sheaves on a
  compact totally-disconnected space, Cantor/ℚ/irrationals). Needs all of the above plus
  Appendix-A point-set topology.

These are flagged here rather than stubbed, per the repo's integrity rule (no `: True`, no vacuous
existence witnesses).
