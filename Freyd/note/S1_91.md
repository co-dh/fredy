### The reduction and the REAL remaining gap (earlier note was wrong)

`apply hm` (right-cancel the monic `g`) reduces `g ≫ g = id` to `g ≫ g ≫ g = g` (`g³ = g`).
More usefully, via `omega_endo_eq_id_of_classifies_true` it suffices to prove `ĝ²(S) = S` for every
subobject `S` (where `ĝ(S) := classify⁻¹(χ_S ≫ g)`); taking `A = Ω, S = id` gives `g ≫ g = id`.

Freyd's operation is `ĝ(S) = (S ⇔ A×U)` for a subterminal `U ⊆ 1` (the Heyting double-arrow of `S`
with the inverse image `A×U = (term_A)# U`), and `g` monic forces `ĝ` to be involutive.

**CORRECTION to the previous claim.** The earlier note said the residual is `U = 1` and that the
collapse is the tautology `(p ↔ (p∧u)) ∧ (p∧u) = p` holding iff `u = ⊤`. That is WRONG on two counts:

1. `U = 1` is FALSE in general. `U` is the unique `g`-large subobject of `1`; `U = 1` ⟺ `t ≫ g = t`
   (`⊤` is a `g`-fixed point). For `g = ¬` in a Boolean topos — where `¬` IS monic and IS a genuine
   involution — the unique `g`-large subobject of `1` is `∅`, so `U = ∅ ≠ 1`. The prior note conflated
   `U` with Freyd's `V` (it is `V = 1` that is the vacuous constraint, not `U`).
2. The operation is `ĝ(S) = (S ⇔ A×U)`, not `(S ↔ A×U) ∧ (A×U)`; the spurious `∧ (A×U)` collapses
   `g = ¬` to `⊥` (wrong). Checked against the two monic endos of `2` (Boolean `Sub`): `g = id` needs
   `U = 1` and `g = ¬` needs `U = ∅`, and `ĝ(p) = (p ⇔ u)` reproduces both (`p⇔⊤ = p`, `p⇔⊥ = ¬p`).

The TRUE missing content is an internal-logic layer, NOT a one-step cancellation:
- (a) build `ĝ(S) = (S ⇔ A×U)` as a `Sub(A)`-operation: inverse-image subterminal `A×U` (char map
  `term_A ≫ χ_U` via `classify_invImg`) fed with `χ_S` into the ⇔-UMP — AND first identify the given
  `g` with `⇔(−, U)` (i.e. show the subobject `g` classifies is the equalizer of `id_Ω` and
  `term_Ω ≫ χ_U`), which is itself not derivable from the present API;
- (b) the Heyting identity `g monic ⟹ ((S ⇔ u) ⇔ u) = S`. In a non-Boolean topos `⇔` is not
  associative and `(x ⇔ u) ⇔ u = x` FAILS pointwise; it holds only because monicity makes
  `S ↦ (S ⇔ u)` injective on `Sub(A)`. Lifting injective ⟹ involutive to an internal Heyting lemma
  over arbitrary `Sub(A)` is the research-grade core.

Subobject-injectivity of `g` is indeed free (`χ₁ ≫ g = χ₂ ≫ g ⟹ χ₁ = χ₂` from `Mono g`), but it is
only one input to (b), not the whole gap. Do NOT re-attempt as a one-liner `U = 1` step.

### The construction (all proven, no new sorry)

- `fullRel A C : BinRel 𝒞 A C` — the "full" relation `M_{A,C}` tabulated by the product projection
  (table `A×C`, columns `fst`, `snd`); jointly monic via `fst_snd_jointly_monic`.
- `fullRel_pullback : f(M_{A,C}) ≅ M_{A',C}` — pulling the full relation back along `f : A' → A` is

- `fullClassify_const : g ≫ Λ(M_{A,B}) = Λ(M_{X,B})` — constancy of `Λ(M_{−,B})`: naturality +
  `fullRel_pullback`.
- Terminator: `e := Λ(M_{[B],B}) : [B] → [B]` is constant; `T := equalizer (id_{[B]}, e)`.
  - `trm A`: `Λ(M_{A,B})` equalizes `id`/`e` (since `Λ(M_{A,B}) ≫ e = Λ(M_{A,B})` by constancy), so it
