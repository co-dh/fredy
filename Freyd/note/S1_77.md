## Keystone: reflexive-transitive closure R* (PRIMITIVE vs DERIVED)

**Verdict: R\* is DERIVED, not a book primitive.** Freyd (§1.77) is explicit: R^t and R* are defined
"if such exists"; they exist precisely in structured categories (transitive logos §1.77, locally
complete §1.773, ω-transitive §1.772, topos §1.947). He never posits R* on a bare regular category.

This file therefore provides BOTH layers, faithfully:

- **The construction (§1.787/§1.947 route):** `transRefClos_of_quotClos` builds a genuine
  `TransRefClos R` from `QuotClos R` (= Freyd's R̄ = ⋂F, the glb of reflexive S with R⊚S ⊑ S) plus
  the self-quotient R̄/R̄ (which a logos has, §1.784). `transRefClos_of_glb_preclosed` is the §1.947
  packaging matching the topos `⋂F` shape. No new axiom — `QuotClos` and `RelQuot` are exactly
  Freyd's data. The key non-circular lemma is `quotClos_self_transitive`.
- **The structure (`HasReflTransClosure`):** a category-level class positing every endo-rel has a
  `TransRefClos`, with `rtc R` and the four reusable lemmas `le_rtc`, `rtc_reflexive`,
  `rtc_transitive`, `rtc_minimal` — the interface downstream consumes.

**Downstream unblocked (identified, not edited — those files are owned by other agents):**

- §1.94/§1.95 `topos_has_rtc`: build M=⋂F (refl, R⊚M⊑M, glb) and M/M, call
  `transRefClos_of_glb_preclosed` → R*. Replaces the family-glb `sorry` once §1.54 capitalization
  supplies the glb M.
- §1.84 `coequalizer_is_coequalizer_in_Rel`: needs R* = ⋃ₙSⁿ; in a `HasReflTransClosure` (or
  ω-transitive) category use `rtc S` + `rtc_transitive`/`rtc_reflexive` instead of building the
  countable union by hand.
- §1.64 / §1.95 `topos_has_coequalizers`: the equivalence closure (R∪R°)* is `rtc (R ∪ᵣ R°)`;
  `equivClos_from_symm_transRefClos` already turns any `TransRefClos` of the symmetrisation into an
  `EquivClos`, so `HasReflTransClosure` ⟹ all equivalence closures.

Note: the ω-union route (⋃ₙSⁿ, §1.771/1.772) is NOT derivable in a bare pre-logos — it needs
countable unions preserved by inverse images, which Freyd flags as an extra hypothesis. That stays
posited in `OmegaTransitive*` (already present); it is genuinely category-structure, not an axiom
we may discharge.

## Summary of sorries

ALL 3 original sorry sites discharged. Zero `sorry` tokens remain in `Freyd/S1_77.lean`.

1. `equivClos_from_symm_transRefClos` (§1.775) — DONE.
   - Symmetry of Rsym*: proved via minimality of Rsym* applied to (Rsym*)°. (Rsym*)° is
     reflexive (`graph(id) ⊑ (graph id)° ⊑ (Rsym*)°`), transitive (`comp_reciprocal_le` +
     reciprocal-monotone on `hr.trans`), and ⊇ Rsym (`Rsym° ⊑ Rsym ⊑ Rsym*`, reciprocate),
     giving `Rsym* ⊑ (Rsym*)°`; reciprocate + involution to flip to `(Rsym*)° ⊑ Rsym*`.
   - Minimality `Rsym ⊑ E`: the original signature was genuinely too weak — `Rsym` abstract
     with only `R ⊑ Rsym` + symmetry cannot force `Rsym ⊑ E`. Added a faithful `hJoin`
     hypothesis (Rsym is the join of R, R°, 1, i.e. its universal property as the
     symmetrisation). With `hJoin` the proof is immediate from `hRE`, E-symmetry, E-reflexivity.
   - Caveat: transitively depends on `sorryAx` only through the cited §1.561 lemma
     `comp_reciprocal_le` (sorried upstream in S1_56, out of scope). No sorry in this file.

2. `quotClos_is_transitive` + `quotClos_eq_transRefClos` (§1.787) — DONE, fully sorry-free
   (axioms = {Classical.choice}, no sorryAx).
   ROOT CAUSE of the old blocker: the previous attempt used R/R (`RelQuot R R`), which forces
   the step `R·(R/R)·R ⊑ R·R` and then stalls needing `R·R ⊑ R` (R not transitive). The book's
   §1.787 FIRST half avoids R/R entirely: it uses the SELF-quotient S/S with S = R̄. Then
   `R ⊑ S/S` is free from `R·S ⊑ S` (= qBar.stable), S/S is reflexive+transitive (§1.786),
   so `R* ⊑ S/S` by R*-minimality, whence `R* ⊑ R̄`. Combined with `R̄ ⊑ R*` this gives R̄ = R*,
   and transitivity of R̄ follows from transitivity of R*.
   Signature change: replaced parameter `qRR : RelQuot R R` with `hr : TransRefClos R` and
   `qSS : RelQuot R̄ R̄` (the self-quotient; exists in a logos by §1.784). This is faithful —
   the theorem statement is about R̄ given that R* exists, exactly the book's setting.
