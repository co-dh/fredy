## Notes

- The book's §1.32 full-cancellation says "if A→B→C is full then G is full"; we prove this
  only on the image of F (`full_of_comp_full_on_image`), which is the correct general statement
  without surjectivity of F on objects.

- `StrongEquivalence` stores only the two nat-isos (existence); the triangle identities are not
  asserted. Despite this, all theorems are proved fully: fullness of F (resp. G) is derived
  by lifting through G∘F fullness and cancelling with G's (resp. F's) embedding property.

- The axiom of choice and §1.362 (every equiv. functor is half of a strong equivalence)
  are mentioned in the book but out of scope for this section.
