## Proof strategy (§1.399)

Routed through componentwise conjugation rather than the book's mapping-cylinder construction.
The key lemma `composeComposablePath_natIso` shows that the `F₂`-image of any composable path
equals the `F₁`-image pre/post-composed by `α`'s boundary inverse `αinv(interp(src))` and
`α(interp(tgt))`.  Because both sides of every Q-equation share the same source/target objects
(via `hSrc`/`hTgt`), they get conjugated by the *same* boundary isos, so the `F₁`-equation
(`sat₁`) transfers directly to the `F₂`-equation.  The composability transport `hab ▸` inside
`composeComposablePath`'s recursion is discharged by the dedicated `subst`-based transport lemmas
`transp_natIsoInv_comp` / `transp_comp_right` (which push the object equality through the
conjugation pattern), avoiding the `cases hab` failure on the non-variable equation `Q.tgt a = Q.src b`.
